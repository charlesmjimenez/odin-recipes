Adobo

Description

Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by simmering meat in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and pepper. It has a rich, savory, and slightly tangy flavor, and is one of the most iconic and beloved meals in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 onion, chopped (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Salt and sugar to taste

Steps

  1. In a pot, combine meat, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Place the pot over medium heat and bring to a boil. Do not stir until it boils.
  3. Add water and simmer until meat is tender (about 30–40 minutes).
  4. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté onion (if using), then brown the meat slightly.
  5. Return meat to the pot, adjust seasoning with salt and a bit of sugar if desired.
  6. Simmer until sauce thickens slightly. Serve with rice.
